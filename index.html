About BRIGHT Lab

The BRIGHT (Biophotonics Research In Intelligent Global Health Technologies) laboratory is dedicated to the development and clinical translation of advanced biophotonic technologies. Our research focuses on Raman spectroscopy, label-free imaging, artificial intelligence-driven virtual histology, and cancer diagnostics.

Under the leadership of Dr. Rishikesh Pandey, we work at the intersection of photonics, biomedical engineering, and artificial intelligence to develop real-time, label-free diagnostic techniques and intraoperative guidance tools in oncology.

Research Focus

🔬

Raman Spectroscopy

Advanced vibrational spectroscopic techniques for molecular characterization and disease diagnosis.

🖼️

Label-Free Imaging

Non-invasive imaging modalities that eliminate the need for staining or contrast agents.

🤖

AI-Driven Histology

Machine learning algorithms for virtual histopathology and automated diagnosis.

🏥

Cancer Diagnostics

Real-time diagnostic tools for cancer detection and intraoperative surgical guidance.

Principal Investigator (PI)

Dr. Rishikesh Pandey

Professor (Assistant- Sr Grade)

Department of Biosciences & Bioengineering
Indian Institute of Technology Roorkee

Dr. Rishikesh Pandey is currently serving as an Assistant Professor (since July 30, 2024) in the Department of Biosciences & Bioengineering at the Indian Institute of Technology Roorkee, where he leads the BRIGHT (Biophotonics Research In Intelligent Global Health Technologies) laboratory.

His research centers on the development and clinical translation of advanced biophotonic technologies, with a focus on Raman spectroscopy, label-free imaging, artificial intelligence-driven virtual histology, and cancer diagnostics.

Dr. Pandey obtained his Ph.D. in Raman Spectroscopy from the Indian Institute of Science and subsequently held postdoctoral positions at the University of Pittsburgh and the G. R. Harrison Spectroscopy Lab at MIT. Before joining IIT Roorkee, he served as Chief Scientist at CytoVeris Inc. (2019–2024) and as affiliate Assistant Professor at the University of Connecticut School of Medicine and Biomedical Engineering.

With a prolific publication record and over 4000+ citations on Google Scholar (h-index ≈27), Dr. Pandey is internationally recognized for his contributions to translating vibrational spectroscopic techniques toward real-time, label-free diagnosis and intraoperative guidance in oncology.
